# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

StitchAndSpec serves confused sewing and embroidery machine buyers who are trying to choose the right machine for the work they actually make: beginners, quilters, denim and canvas sewists, cosplayers, monogrammers, alteration sewists, and small-business embroiderers. They are often preparing to spend roughly $500–$2,000 and feel anxious about choosing wrong, outgrowing a hoop size, buying decorative features instead of useful capability, or discovering too late that a machine cannot handle their material or workflow.

## Product Purpose

StitchAndSpec is an independent sewing and embroidery machine research site. Its single job is to help a buyer choose the right machine for their real projects by translating specs into practical decisions: hoop size, fabric handling, stitch control, stabilizer, service access, upgrade risk, and workflow fit. Success means the reader leaves with less anxiety, a clearer shortlist, and a next practical question instead of hype.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, calm, expert, and genuinely useful. The voice should feel like a knowledgeable friend who knows machines inside out: precise without being cold, warm without being sentimental, and practical without sounding like a spec database or affiliate pitch.

Concrete brand-voice words: tactile, methodical, reassuring.

## Anti-references

Do not make StitchAndSpec look like a hype-driven affiliate blog, a generic SEO content farm, a cold spec database, or a templated AI landing page. Avoid the saturated AI defaults: warm cream/beige body backgrounds by reflex, serif-plus-terracotta editorial layouts, near-black with acid accent, hairline broadsheet grids, identical card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section, arbitrary numbered markers, over-rounded ghost cards, gradient text, glassmorphism, decorative sketchy SVGs, and empty text-only pages with no imagery.

## Design Principles

1. **Project before product.** Every visual and structural choice should remind the reader that the right machine depends on the work: shirts, quilts, bags, hats, alterations, cosplay, or small-business batches.
2. **Material truth over decoration.** Draw character from the real sewing world: fabric swatches, thread color, stitch paths, hoop boundaries, pattern tables, machine photo slots, and annotation labels. Do not use generic tech gloss.
3. **Calm confidence.** The page should lower purchase anxiety with clear hierarchy, readable line lengths, direct answers, and visible source trails.
4. **Guided research path.** Each page should feel like part of a buyer journey: answer the current question, show the decision table, then point to the next question.
5. **Editorial craft without magazine cosplay.** This is a content-first publication, but not a broadsheet fashion spread. Use warmth, texture, and useful imagery rather than hairline newspaper affectation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA: body text contrast at least 4.5:1, large text at least 3:1, visible keyboard focus, semantic landmarks, readable mobile type, no horizontal overflow, and touch targets at least 44px where practical. Respect reduced-motion preferences. Do not communicate important distinctions by color alone; support them with labels, structure, and copy.
